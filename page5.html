Backups

Rob approached me midway through week 12 of the 14 of the semester, letting me know he had a project in mind. He needed me to look into a backup system that would then later be implemented by the Project team next semester. We discussed the options with that and I agreed to have a proposal written up. I'd need to understand what people were working on and how much storage would be needed before I could come back with an answer.

I sent out a Teams message asking the group to have a think about it and that I'd get in touch over the next few days to find out what they needed. I suspect a lot of groups are already implementing backup systems currently, which means it may be as simple and congregating them into a single hub. 

In my personal experience with backups, I have primarily relied on Azure's on board systems and they're relatively hassle free. Additionally, I've done research into Azure Storage Blobs after discussing them with fellow Project student Jordan. I suspect that these solutions will prove too costly in bulk.

I suspect that the solution will be a single backups VM which is then also backed up with Azure backups. This way we're only paying out a single manageable cost as well as having a unified best practice.
